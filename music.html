My Hobby

Music Listening 🎧

For me, music is far more than just background noise; it is a universal language and a powerful tool for personal growth. As a passionate listener, I have always been fascinated by how lyrics tell a story, and actively listening to global songs is actually one of the primary ways I have learned and improved my English. Tuning into different genres has helped me understand the rhythm, vocabulary, and nuances of the language in a way that traditional textbooks never could, proving that a great melody can bridge any linguistic divide.

While my taste spans globally, the absolute heart of my musical journey lies in Indian cinema, especially the rich, soul-stirring world of Tamil film music. I am a massive fan of deep, emotional melodies, which has drawn me to the legendary work of iconic music directors. The timeless orchestrations of Isaignani Ilayaraja, the global soundscapes of AR Rahman, the infectious rhythms of Harris Jayaraj, and the atmospheric, modern soul of Yuvan Shankar Raja form the soundtrack to my life. Their compositions come alive through the flawless voices of my favorite playback singers, SP Balasubrahmanyam and Shreya Ghoshal, whose incredible vocal ranges and emotive expressions can move me to tears or instantly lift my spirit.

Ultimately, my deep connection to music is rooted in the immense happiness and positivity it brings to my daily life. Whether I am listening to a classic 80s melody or a modern cinematic masterpiece, a good song has the undeniable power to shift my mindset, heal my stress, and offer comfort. It keeps me open-minded, creative, and constantly inspired by human expression, serving as a reminder that no matter where we come from, rhythm and emotion are things we all share.

Where words fail, music speaks.
